Behind the Data

About Me

I got into this by taking messy, half broken datasets and turning them into something a business could actually act on. Along the way I started automating the boring parts too, things like triggering ingestion jobs or sending out alerts, because running the same steps by hand every day felt like a waste of time.

I care more about being able to explain my work than making it look polished. If something is on my resume or in a project writeup, I can walk you through how I got there, the code behind it and where it falls short.

The part I keep coming back to is automation. I don't just want to analyze data, I want to build workflows that do the repetitive work for me, from ingest triggers to alert pipelines. I think analysts who understand this side of things end up being more useful.
